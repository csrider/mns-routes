Graphical Annunciator

MessageNet Connections Manual

Overview & Graphical Annunciator Concepts:
  • A graphical annunciator (MediaPort Panel GA) is able to display safety-related devices (like smoke detectors, fire pull stations, fire extinguishers, etc.) and their current state on a map (similar to Routing functionality)
    • Properly placed safety devices are able to be displayed on the map, and also indicate their current state upon an alarm event (automatic message launch) or manual message launch.
    • You must enter the Graphical Annunciator management screen through a message definition/editor screen - NOT the locations screen. Remember that in order to display a GA screen on a MediaPort Panel GA, you will be defining a GRAPHICAL ANNUNCIATOR type message.
  • The system also supports multiple floorplan or map graphics, each with their own potential set of device icons. Even though a single LCD-GA device, by its nature, is only capable of showing one graphic at a time (full screen), additional map graphics can be associated with a given message, and be told whether to display (active) or not (inactive). This allows a full range of flexibility in how you create and manage all of your possible maps and devices through any situation.
    • You may need to associate maps with a message (especially if it is brand new). This can be done by selecting from already-loaded system maps, or by uploading your own map, via the button.
Security Considerations and Access to Graphical Annunciator Configuration:
  • To gain access, you MUST be a member of the "SYS ROUTING CONTROL" user list.
  • Being a member of the "SYS LOCATIONS ACCESS LIST" or any other "LOCATIONS"-type of list will NOT gain you access.
  • The device(s) that the message will be sent to must be of the "MediaPort Panel GA" type.
Message Requirements When Configuring Graphical Annunciators:
  • Create/save a message, as you would any normal message to an LCD device, but with the following special considerations:
    • Be sure that the Sign Group contains at least one device of the "MediaPort Panel GA" type.
    • Select a Media Type of "LCD Location Map".
    • Select an Alert Status of "Graphical Annunciator."
  • Once the proper Media Type has been defined, and if you have the proper user list clearance, there will be a button available after saving the message, labeled "Edit Map Features." You may then click that button to enter the Graphical Annunciator management screen for the particular message.
Accessing the Graphical Annunciator Management Screen:

After clicking in the message editor screen (only available if you have proper security clearance), you will be taken to a screen looking very much like the usual Locations screen. If you make any device changes here, be sure to click to save your changes, just to be safe!

You may move, or place device icons and assign their previously defined names to them (see help for Locations - it is the same process).

Tip: You may simply move your mouse cursor over most buttons or icons to get an instant help pop-up explaining its status or function. This will be your best bet for understanding the options available in this tool!

Graphical Annunciator Management Screen - NO Map(s) Associated With Message Yet:
  • Depending on your security clearance, you may or may not see any of these icons.
  • Click this button to associate a map with the message (you may use an existing map that's in the system, or you may upload a new map altogether).
Graphical Annunciator Management Screen - Map(s) Associated With Message:
  • Click any of these icons to place a safety device icon on the map (do not drag, just click).
  • Click to enable editing of a device icon that's already placed on the map (first click this, then click the icon you wish to edit).
  • Click to remove a device icon from the map (first click this, then click the icon you wish to remove).
  • Click to save any changes made to the screen.
  • Click to choose an active/default map for a safety device icon (first click this, then click a safety device icon). If a safety device goes into alarm, and it is represented by an icon in this system, it's possible to display it (on its map) on a MediaPort GA display. This button allows you to manage what map will display on the LCD, in the case that a safety icon happens to be placed on multiple maps (an LCD can only display one map per message).
  • GA Default:Check this box to make the current map the default for the message.
  • Click this button to associate additional map(s) with the message.
  • Click this button to dis-associate the current map from the message (this will not delete the map from the system).
  • Click this button to edit certain properties of the current map.
